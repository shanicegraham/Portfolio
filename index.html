About Me

A legal mind turned UX/UI designer

I am a chartered legal executive turned UX/UI designer. I built my career in law solving real problems with structure, evidence, and empathy. That experience trained me to untangle messy journeys, communicate with stakeholders, and turn constraints into clear, actionable decisions. The same discipline now powers my UX practice, where I apply rigorous research, information architecture, and prototyping to bring empathy, evidence, and sharp execution to every brief.

I love turning ambiguity into clarity from end to end. I plan with user journeys, information architecture and prototype in Figma, create brand and visual assets, validate through testing, and refine until the experience fits the users needs and brief. My legal background makes me comfortable with managing stakeholders, documenting complex processes and communicating clearly under pressure. My design mindset keeps me curious, collaborative, and focused on outcomes.

I excel in multidisciplinary teams whether aligning product, business, and technical perspectives or co-creating solutions in design sprints. My experience managing clients and internal stakeholders in fast paced environments has made me confident in navigating feedback, facilitating discussions and building consensus.

I am enthusiastic about service and product challenges where users face jargon, long forms, or fragmented processes. I translate rules into usable flows, simplify content, and design interfaces that keep the user at the focus.

Outside of work, you'll often find me on a Pilates mat, baking or catching up with family and friends, ideally at a restaurant with great food and conversation. I bring the same warmth and curiosity to those moments that I bring to my design work.
